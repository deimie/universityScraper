Minimum GPA: 2.00 overall college GPA (California residents for Upper and Lower Division Transfer); 2.25 overall GPA for selection into the Through-in-Two Program.
Application Deadlines: FALL 2026: Nursing majors - December 1, 2025. All other non-impacted majors - March 2, 2026 (Priority Deadline). SPRING 2026: All non-impacted programs - December 5, 2025 (Extended Deadline). Nursing not available in Spring.
Required Tests: SAT/ACT - Not Applicable (Discontinued for undergraduate admissions). NURSING ONLY - ATI Test of Essential Academic Skills (TEAS) with a minimum National Percentile overall rank of proficient (>58.7%).
Transfer Pathways: Associate Degree for Transfer (ADT) (AA-T/AS-T) accepted; guarantees a pathway to graduation in 60 units *at CI* if major is similar.
Application Components Required: Essays/Personal Insight Questions (PIQs) - None (not typically required for CSU transfer). Letters of Recommendation (LORs) - None (not typically required for general CSU transfer). Portfolio/Interview - None (for general admission). NURSING ONLY - Supplemental NursingCAS application is required, and a supplemental criteria point system is used for admission, which considers health-related experience (e.g., LVN, CNA, EMT), military experience, second language proficiency, and high school health academy participation.
General Education Certification: IGETC or CSU GE-Breadth Certification is accepted. Upper-Division Transfers (60+ units) must have the "Golden Four" completed with a grade of C- or better: A1 (Oral Communication), A2 (Written Communication), A3 (Critical Thinking), and B4 (Mathematics/Quantitative Reasoning).
Maximum Transferable Units: Upper-Division Transfer students must have completed a minimum of 60 semester transferable units (90 quarter units). No explicit maximum transferable units found, but ADT students are guaranteed a 60-unit graduation path.
Residency Requirements: Students in the Through-in-Two Program must complete a minimum of 30 units each academic year (including summer) to ensure graduation in two years; residency requirements must be fulfilled by graduation.
