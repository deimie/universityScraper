--- GENERAL_INFO_START ---
Minimum GPA: 2.0 minimum overall GPA in all transferable college courses (Higher GPA is required for the impacted BS Nursing major and may be needed for general admission if applicant pool is competitive).
Application Deadlines: Fall Priority Filing Period: October 1 - November 30. Spring Deadlines: Varies, dependent on campus space availability (check Cal State Apply for open terms).
Required Tests: None (CSU does not use SAT/ACT for transfer admission decisions).
Transfer Pathways: Associate Degree for Transfer (ADT - AA-T/AS-T) is accepted and provides an admission guarantee to the CSU system (not a specific campus or impacted major).
General Education Certification: Intersegmental General Education Transfer Curriculum (IGETC) or CSU General Education Breadth Certification is accepted to fulfill lower-division GE requirements.
Maximum Transferable Units: A maximum of 70 semester (105 quarter) units from a community college may be transferred toward a CSUCI bachelor's degree.
Residency Requirements: A minimum of 50 university units must be completed at CSUCI (non-community college units). This includes a minimum of 12 upper-division units in the major and a minimum of 24 upper-division units total.
Estimated Annual Cost (Tuition + Fees): California Resident (full-time, 6.1+ units) is approximately $7,159 for the 2024-2025 academic year ($6,084 Systemwide Tuition Fee + $1,075 CSUCI Campus Fees). Nonresident students pay an additional $444 per unit.
Transfer-Specific Scholarships: CSUCI institutional transfer-specific scholarships are N/A. Students should pursue external awards such as the Phi Theta Kappa (PTK) Scholarship.
--- MAJOR_START ---
Major Name: Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BS Nursing)
Major Selectivity: Impacted
Required Lower-Division Courses: Human Anatomy with Lab; Human Physiology with Lab; Microbiology with Lab; Chemistry (Intro or General); Statistics; Oral Communication (CSU GE A1); Written Communication (CSU GE A2); Critical Thinking (CSU GE A3).
Minimum Grade: "C" or better in all prerequisite courses is the minimum. Historically, competitive applicants to impacted CSU Nursing programs require a higher GPA (often 3.0+ overall and a 3.0+ in science prerequisites).
